## Chapter 3: The Tenant

As a tenant residing inside Isaac’s consciousness, I behaved properly during his tenancy, but it was extremely difficult to resist  intruding into Isaac’s stream of consciousness, especially if he was thinking in the wrong direction. But that  was the fate of a passive observer, to silently watch while Rome burns.


But at night when Isaac slept,  I would wander out of his nook on my  own and investigate Jason’s Virtual Reality world to see if I could find Jason there.  And because I  was familiar with being lost as a natural state of being, it wasn’t long before I found Jason’s world, only after two attempts. Jason’s world in the  virtual world was built according to his own constructs. I was surprised when  confronted by someone there,  when I felt a tap on my shoulder.


“Who are you?” a voice from behind said. I turned around to see a seventeen year old boy.


“Daneel,” I responded.


“I built this house myself, you know…based on Dad’s lab in the real world.  And of course everything works here as it should.  Excruciatingly difficult… but I was able to assemble the whole thing in two days.  I call it Jason’s Nook. What are you doing anyway in the VR world?” Jason remarked..


I couldn’t tell him I was a tenant in his father’s body, so I said “I’m your Dad’s friend.  I’ll tell him I met you here if you don’t mind.”


And the boy turned away, back to his machines. ”It’s alright, he doesn’t have time for me anyway…”, he muttered, totally absorbed in what he was doing.


What a treat! I had found Jason, or a VR version of him anyway. I had to keep it secret to myself for now, lest Jason did  not want to be found.  Funny how humans value privacy above all. Oh well, follow and conform to known rules!


Back inside Isaac's mind , I watch in silence his activities through the years, as he tries to resolve his original problem of how to persist a human consciousness into an android. I never offered unsolicited advice, Isaac never needed it anyway.  I was thereto watch Isaac’s wins when he succeeded and to suffer with him the frustration when he could not solve an issue. But when he resolved a persistent issue, the joy he derived from it overflowed. In success, I see him exude waves of happiness as ripples of blue.  And when he sulks in frustration, I see drips of deep crimson and red ripples. 


It had been six years since the tenancy started. Throughout that time, I noticed Isaac's gradual mental decline, even his speech acquired a slight slur.  At the time  when he seemed to  be fading away, I told him of my encounter with Jason. Then in a flash  of inspiration, I wore Jason's brain entrainment headset and guided Isaac to Jason's Nook. 


"Hi, Dad... So you finally found time to visit me here!  I built a lab just like yours here. It may even  be better," muttered Jason.


"Jason!" exclaimed Isaac. Father and son reunions could be so emotional, for both. The two were overjoyed at each other's presence. Now they could research together...in perpetuity.


Back into Isaac’s body - but he was gone.  It was a bit different now without Isaac.  Somehow the body felt heavier with just me at the controls. I thought:  so this is how it feels to be at the forefront, controlling the body.  There was a subtle difference - an extra spring in the step was previously there when Isaac was in control. 



Isaac’s body was heavy. It wears me down. I had forgotten meals the past three days. Even constant rehydration became a chore.  Having a host meant I didn’t have to concern myself with these trivialities.  It was second nature to the host.  Maybe Jason was right -choosing to live in the VR world, where the biological body  was not a  factor.  I tried living in the VR world, but something was missing…  what are the significant events?  Nothing ever happens to anyone.  After a week of dragging along Isaac’s body, I had to let go of his body.  Too heavy. The ambulance came to the door at 6:30 a.m. and I was ready for the ride. When they rushed me to the Emergency Ward and tried to revive me, I quickly transferred to one of the comatose victims, thinking it was easy picking. But I was dead wrong.
