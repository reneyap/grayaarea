## Chapter 2: The Rubensteins { 2023-2080,...}


Isaac had always been a loner.  As a child, he would rather stay in his room, picking apart his old broken toys, driven by curiosity as to what made his toys work the way they do. Putting back the parts after disassembly was a different matter altogether, though. Although he had a  photographic memory and could reassemble his toys back to their previous state, where was the fun in that?  His favorite pastime was to mix and match parts of one toy with another and create a whole new toy with hybrid features. It felt  good being Dr. Frankenstein, manufacturing new creations. 


It was this penchant  for disassembly and reassembly which brought him to his current expertise,  Robotic Data Science. As a child prodigy, he entered  university life at age fifteen and finished his degree in Artificial Intelligence - Applied  Robotic Data Science in 3 years. Onwards to a Masters in Electronic Neural Science while his old university classmates had to slave it out for another 2 years just to get a college degree. At age 22, he had already earned his PhD in Advance Heuristics and Decision Simulation from the prestigious Harry Sheldon Institute in Switzerland.  Only gifted scholars were invited to study in HSI.  


Being top of the class in every educational Institute he studied ensured that Isaac was granted the most coveted Harry Sheldon Scholarship, wherein you are paid to study in Europe with the most brilliant minds of that era.  It was here that he met Alice,  the smartest mathematician and expert in Probability Factor Analysis. She wasn't particularly pretty when he first met her, a flat-chested girl wearing horn-rimmed glasses, complete with untidy, rumpled hair.  She was brilliant and fearless, though. 


Isaac remembered the first time he proposed a theory in Heuristics to Professor Hoffenstein during class and was immediately shot down with an impatient smirk from Alice, who grudgingly went up to the blackboard to write down the mathematical equation that disproved  the possibility of his theorem. And true enough, Alice was right.  The premise wasn’t possible in the first place. Isaac stood  corrected, literally, and for the first time. He had never been wrong in his academic life yet. But here it was, Isaac was proven wrong. The professor was about to nod and agree with Isaac’s Theory of Heuristic Relativity when he was rudely shut down by Alice. The air-tight argument against that theory as presented by Alice spoke for itself.  Isaac immediately fell in love with Alice at that moment. 


Finally….here was someone who understood his thoughts and was  fearless in pointing out all his erroneous preconceptions.  From that point onwards, Isaac and Alice were inseparable. She became his sounding board.  Together they were a formidable  team. He tested his theories on Robotic Logic often and was amazed at the clarity of her thoughts.  She was instrumental in proving Isaac’s Law of  Robotic Logic. He also noticed that Alice began wearing perfume and makeup. Gone were the glasses and disheveled  look. A new Alice emerged in the succeeding semesters. Shorter skirts and body hugging dresses replaced the shabby jumpsuits she usually wore. 


After a year of courtship, it was only natural that they got married in the Spring of 2045. All was well after the birth of their son Jason. They raised him up with deep affection. Jason, like Isaac, had  a sharp intellect and was as stubborn as Alice.  Even at a young age, Jason had memorized the Periodic Table of Elements, breezed through mathematics at age ten, bored and impatient at his classmates who struggle to understand the simple math progressions and patterns, such as Fibonacci Number sequences, prime numbers, and complex square and cube root sequences.  It was literally child’s play for Jason. He  graduated from high school at age fifteen  and was accepted to the prestigious Kingston Institute of Advanced Science. 


His emotional state was erratic though.  He had frequent bouts of depression,  borne out of frustration when he could not solve certain difficult theories and principles. At times like these, it was difficult to pry Jason out of his shell. Reclusive and unresponsive, Jason would lash out in anger when anyone tried to coax him out of his self-imposed silence. 


It was the year 2060 when Jason got his brain entrainment headgear, he became entrenched in Virtual Reality Digital Trance.  Most teenagers his age were playing games and meeting friends in the Virtual Reality world. Jason became addicted to the popular Brain Entertainment Adventure which sapped him of his innate intelligence. After being captivated for two straight months in the Brain Wave world,  he completely lost interest in pursuing his Biometrics Degree in Augmented Mechanics.  


Isaac noticed that Jason had changed completely,  becoming more withdrawn and unresponsive. He hardly came back to reality anymore and was mentally absorbed in the VR world. Jason became physically emaciated  and feeble minded over time.  Isaac and  Alice,extremely concerned with his well-being, enlisted the services of eminent psychologist Kevin Lemurr to extract Jason out of the Virtual Reality world.  After a year of therapy, it was apparent that the whole endeavor was an exercise in futility. The Jason they had raised was lost inside the complex world of augmented reality. What remained of Jason was an empty shell, locked in a vegetative state. Eventually Jason stopped eating all together and wasted away. Alice fell into a deep depression after Jason faded away and left the world at age 17. She filed for divorce from Isaac soon after, blaming Isaac for having not prevented  Jason from slipping into the unknown world of Virtual Reality.  Her world crumbled and shattered; something in her died with Jason.


The only thing that Isaac kept was Jason’s entrainment headgear which became the inspiration of the first prototype to his positronic headgear
