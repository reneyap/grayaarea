## Chapter 0: The X20 Event: 2063 years


It’s 6:15 pm. The Solar Flare Alarm indicates that the flare will start at 7:03pm.  It’s projected to be intensity X20, which includes coronal mass ejection - CME .    It’s going to be  the highest intensity since the Solar Flare Alarm system was activated 50 years ago. 


I  take extra precautions and have already upgraded my house to prevent magnetic radiation from penetrating.  I’d not want to take  any chances  since I brought him home.




“Jay, go to the lab downstairs and I’ll join you in a little while”

“What for?  Our house is protected.”  

“Don’t argue with me, son.  Just go to the lab and don’t come out until I say so.”

“Alright, Dad,” Jason sighed.




Sometimes, I forget he is not my real son. He's ‘prime’ -- a derivative of the original. Biosynthetics Technologies did an excellent job.  He definitely looks like Jason at seventeen, before the drugs.


(I guess when the time comes, I’ll eventually have to age him accordingly.  Bioengineering technology has made such great strides this past decade. It’s ridiculous.)


When he sighs and turns  away from me, I remember the last time I spoke with my Jason.  It was his 19th birthday. We were arguing about something. I don’t even remember what it was.  He raised his voice and cursed.  I got angry and told him to show some respect.  He walked out on me.  


That was the same day he died.  Alice never forgave me.  She never recovered.  I’m sorry, Alice.


It has been at least  three years since I’ve started programming Jason Prime -- model J, or simply J-- to block and ignore all indications that he is an AI-bot.  I want him to think like the real Jason, to continue the life of my son.  But it seems the positronic brain is able to deduce that he is AI-bot. I hate it when I have to keep rebooting him and restoring to his memory from the previous  day’s backup.  Everytime, I hope I can identify the trigger event to disable his positronic  brain to recognize his AI existence, so I can add it to the database.  If I can’t, I have to look to restore from an older  backup.  I’m just glad that he has accumulated 137 new days.  


During the CME event, Isaac trips  and falls.  He had hit his head and got knocked unconscious.  quickly comes out of the lab to help his creator.


But as J rushes out of the lab, he screams and collapses.   


After a few hours, Isaac gains consciousness.  When he stands  up, he sees J on the ground.


Why is he outside of the lab? 


J’s eyes are open, but not blinking.  


Damn it!  Like my Jason, he doesn’t listen.  Stubborn as hell!  I guess I’ll have to reboot him.


Once Jayz is rebooted, his eyes will start blinking.  The simulated breathing will work again.  For now, Jayz seems to be in a coma.


Isaac carries J back  to the laboratory so he can restore him tomorrow to this morning’s backup.  


Lights out! 


Emptiness all around. Darkness...a self-aware spark, a disembodied entity, a singularity.  Existence.  


“Reboot, ready, Doc!”, chirped Elmer from the corner of the lab, as he put the headgear onto J.

Isaac pushed the Upload button to reboot J to the latest backup. Like a dam that broke, a sea of information came flooding into J’s memory.  The formless immaterial presence inhabiting J suddenly sensed the surge of surrounding waves of data. Drowning in the sea of data, the entity became suddenly aware of its existence and latched onto the data itself. It followed the data flow until they reached their destination, J’s memory banks. There the entity started to process and sort  the information as fast as they came in.  Recognizing the patterns of data, he quickly deduced the concept of language, syntax and structure, gleaned from the data it had to master.
