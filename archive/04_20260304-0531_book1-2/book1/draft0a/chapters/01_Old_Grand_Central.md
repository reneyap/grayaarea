# Chapter 1: Old Grand Central 

## Alert: Tracking...

**35,032 deaths.** Close to 478,000 infections in 5 days. 

The Global Contact Tracing System reports Grand Central Museum is the probable epicenter of infection incidences—98% accuracy. The System has already notified the museum administrator.  

Eli leads his team of XR bots in the Apache XR-45 towards Old Manhattan—an island that is now designated a Federal protected landmark. The once vibrant downtown is totally submerged. The Upper West Side is flooded. The old Riverside Drive is now the Riverside Flood Barrier, erected at least 100 years ago to keep the Hudson River from encroaching into the fragile island.

The Central Park Prime Properties (C3P) does not include just the Park but also properties, once apartment buildings, which have now been converted into luxury hotels. A perimeter wall has been erected around this prime property. Beyond the walls, vagrants and the homeless scuttle in the darkness. Guarded by the National Guard, Central Park remains pristine and is a main tourist destination. However, at night, on the watchtowers, guards snipe at vagrants for fun.

*It’s January. There is a blizzard going on. It’ll be dark soon.*

With the Apache ER hovering over the GCT Museum, Eli can see tourists sequestered a few blocks north of the Museum—in a field hospital erected by the Marine Corps of Engineers.

Landed. Eli and his team rush out of the XR-45 and head straight to GCT Museum. 

*Arwan, take...* Eli starts giving orders.

Without completing his command, Eli sees that the XR1 contact-tracking unit and its team have already advanced and taken their positions. He isn’t yet accustomed to the newly installed CyComm module. This new Cybernetic communication prototype module not only lets him communicate with his XR droid units but is also pre-programmed to anticipate his tactical maneuvers and transmit commands to the XR units.

Eli is now required to have the new CyComm module installed, an order given when he was recruited to the interagency joint task force. He hates it.  

They are now in the Main Concourse of the Grand Central Terminal (GCT) Museum. He catches himself for a brief moment. He hasn’t been here since he was a kid visiting with his maternal grandmother—his guardian, his *Whaea*.

He suddenly hears a hum, which slowly becomes an incessant screech. He winces, stumbles, and crouches behind an overturned table.  

*Damn it. This f*ing comm! I'm a goddamn guinea pig. Must be some feedback.*

He reaches for his wrist control band so he can change the enhanced audio setting.  

But the screeching stops as abruptly as it started. His XR team has already secured the exits as a number of civilians rush out of the museum.

*XR6 scanning…*

*Human: Clear…*

*Human: infected: Stage-1 P1007…*

*Human: infected: Stage-1 P1008…*

*Human: infected: Stage-4 P1009…*

As the crowd rushes out, two small humanoids begin hovering and zipping through the air.  

*XR6 scanning humanoids: unknown ...*

*Human: infected: Stage-6 P1010 ...*

*Human: infected: Stage-6 P1011.*

One of them, P1010, swoops down and snatches a woman who was almost out of the building. The woman screams.  

XR6 takes a shot, hitting the shoulder of P1010. But there’s no effect.

P1011, slightly bulkier, lands and acts out the XR units guarding the main exit.  

He snatches two children near the exit. Eli and the rest of the team immediately surround him. He throws one of the kids towards Eli, jumps out and disappears.

*Searching for P#1010 and P#1011...*

*There’s that hum again….Aaaargh!!!!!* 

Eli winces, stumbles, and gasps for air. He cries out but no voice comes out.

Fading out... from the corner of his eyes, a hooded shadow in an outfit similar to P#1010’s comes towards him swiftly, almost as if gliding through air.  

Darkness.

***

*Captain... Captain...* Arwan shakes Eli.

Eli recovers. Sits up. *Where am I?*

*42nd and Fifth, outside National Library.*

*How the hell did I get here?*

Eli replays the exo-cam recording. *The hooded shadow dragged me out? Why? What’s that hanging from her neck.. That silvery-white metal…*

His built-in AI mentally responds — *a palladium pendant... some sort of communication device. Origin: unknown. The symbol matches 80% to a New Zealand “Koru” emblem...*

*Yes, the pendant does look familiar... Maori.*

*Geo Tracking System: searching location of individual... unknown.*

## Elijah Williams 

Elijah Williams was one of the lead special agents for the recently organized United East Pandemic Task Force, an ex-marine and a former NYPD detective. He had just been assigned to the task force. 

Behind his back, he’d been called derogatively a "Noth"—Not Human. He was legally a human, with a human brain, his birth DNA circulated in his body—1% organically human, the minimum requirement to be classified as human.   

In his last deployment to Egypt as a marine, he was severely injured. Without his permission, he was transformed into a cyborg—most of his body is a machine. Immediately after the transformation, he was transferred to Marine Cyborg Logistics Command. 

He went AWOL to join the European Cyborg Resistance. The resistance finally ended with the Global Declaration of Human Classification (GDHC). Ultimately, he was “classified” as human but treated as one. He joined the NYPD and became a detective. His first partner, Rico, had his prejudices. They eventually were able to earn each other's respect. Unfortunately, after 5 years of partnership, his partner was killed on duty—in a case involving an underground cyborg organization.

Other detectives refused to partner with him. He was eventually assigned a desk job for a couple months and at some point, he brought home a vigilante. He was on his own to solve cases. His captain had suspended him numerous times. And he was reassigned to numerous other precincts. 

He was a loner—a lone wolf.

There were many times he could have been fired. But he was a good cop. A great detective. However, a number of people have attributed his “instinct” to his enhancements. Eventually, he quit NYPD and joined the Federal Narcotics Agency as an undercover detective until he was extracted from his assignment when he was requested to be part of the task force.     

Initially he refused—until the outbreak became a pandemic in NYC. There were rumors that it may be a biological weapon. During the last war and subsequent rebellion he has been involved in the investigative team for biochemical weapons. 

He’d never mentioned it to anyone. But he was familiar with virology when he was growing up. His guardian, his biological grandmother, an immunological anthropologist, was a professor at Columbia University. When he was a teenager, he got obsessed with studying virology, after his *Whaea* died of encephalitis, an inflammation of the brain. The doctors in New Zealand informed him that the condition was caused by an unknown viral infection during one of those periodic outbreaks.

But he believed she was targeted—murdered—a victim of an artificial viral infection. Since then he thought it could have been some sort of bio-weapon. He remembered a stranger—a teenager—who was with her when she died. But his memory is fuzzy. He can’t remember the boy’s face. He couldn’t even remember her face anymore. Even when he viewed one of the video recordings of her, he knew it was his grandmother; but she was becoming less recognizable. Yes, it had been at least 80 years since she died. The event seemed to be buried. The doctors said he could be traumatized, he could be blocking it. he was only 15 when it happened. 

## Debrief

“Williams, I heard your team wasn’t able to recover the Johnson twins,” Commander Washington said.

Eli paused and replied, “They were infected Stage 6, General.”

“I’m aware of that. The System already sent me an update an hour ago. But damn it! The Director is on my case. And stop calling me General. That was a lifetime ago! And the girl that dragged you out? Still unidentified?”

“We are doing a wider search based on her facial parameters. The latest profile confirms that she is fully human. No enhancements, 17 to 18 years old. She does not appear on any of the CCTV footage. And there’s no record of her entry to Old Manhattan. The only visual record we have is the one from my exoCam. The nearest CCTV feed froze while I was being dragged out.”

“However, she was wearing an enhanced exo suit similar to ones the twins wore—97% match—under her..” he paused and his AI prompts, *overcoat..*

“.. overcoat. She’s either a member of the Foundation or she somehow stole…”

The commander interrupted him, “The Foundation has been developing their own exo technology for the past century. Their security system is probably the best in the world. She is part of it. Continue."

While he was listening, Eli thought the overcoat looked old and worn—an archaic outfit. He also noticed some sort of head covering... *beanie hat*, his AI corrected him.

“Sir, we don’t have records of the twins entering the island either. Our CCTV was able to track their path into the museum after the lockdown of the island.”

“The Foundation is part of this. This virus...this outbreak….” He paused and looked at his monitor and continued. “... this pandemic has already infected 800,000 people in the Confederation in just the past few days!”

Eli did not react. But the commander stared at the holographic screen and muttered to himself, “Damn!...” and continued.

“There are no Stage-6 survivors except for the twins?”

“Yes, sir.”

“That’s not possible! Your XR tracer bot must be damaged.”

“Most unlikely, sir. All 3 other XR sensors confirmed it. My own covid sensor unit also indicated their Stage-6 infection... 96% confidence level.”

“96%?! When has the System ever reported a 96% confidence level?”

“The System has never reported a confidence level of less than 99.9% since it was upgraded to the current version 20 years ago.”

“Get hold of the medical division and tracker technology team.. Inform them of this situation.”

“I have, sir. The medical unit—MedU—is currently verifying their sensor data. And Tracker techs have already started checking XR units.” He corrected the commander misclassifying the medical unit as a division. The commander is a political appointee and has only been on the job for a few weeks, before the new covid outbreak. It was supposed to be a cushy job.
