# Mechanisms: Could Lower Manhattan Sink?

Summary
- Partial, progressive inundation is most plausible; sudden wholesale sinking is unlikely.

Realistic drivers
- Sea-level rise: Antarctic ice loss can add meters over decades.
- Land subsidence: glacial isostatic adjustment, groundwater withdrawal, landfill compaction.
- Infrastructure failure: seawall/pump system failure, saltwater corrosion of foundations.

Acceleration triggers (plausible sci-fi)
- AMOC slowdown: regional sea-level amplification along the U.S. East Coast.
- Rapid ice-shelf or Thwaites collapse: multi-meter rise in decades.
- Catastrophic barrier breach during a superstorm with concurrent grid failure.

Narrative use
- Combine slow processes (sea rise + subsidence) with one acceleration event (storm + CME/X20) to justify a rapid policy decision to retreat.
- Emphasize landfill/liquefaction vulnerability in lower Manhattan locations like Battery Park.
