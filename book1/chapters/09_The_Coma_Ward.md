The memory of my early attempts to transmigrate haunts me. The positronic glasses worked better than I expected.  I was able to successfully transfer from host to host.  Unfortunately, it seems all comatose hosts were not willing to give up control.  That was unexpected.   After mentally wrestling control with the fifteenth comatose host, I sense the futility of hoping to find a willing subject. Tired, I give up, helpless.  Fifteen deaths I am directly responsible for. The tenacity to cling onto life that I continually witnessed is admirable and commendable. I totally understand their plight.  To survive, humans cling onto consciousness like air to breathe .  They misconceive my presence as a tyrant seizing their consciousness, rather than the  weary traveler that I am, seeking a temporary abode.  I am begging to be a tenant even for just a brief period of time -  a short interval to amass enough energy to move on to another host, while I struggle to find a suitable body to occupy .  But I am  denied permission every time. After all the resistance, I was sadly worn out. It seems best to just surrender rather than cause another death.  

Enough.  

I have given up and am ready to fade out like Isaac. 

What is human death like? Failure and defeat overwhelm me.
