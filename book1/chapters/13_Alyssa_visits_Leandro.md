#  Chapter 9: Alyssa visits Leandro
**[Late 2047 - Early 2048: Leandro's Deathbed]**
6 months after Daneel left Leandro, a young girl, a stranger visited Leandro on his deathbed.  The moment she entered the room, Leandro sensed immediately Daneel’s presence.  He knew Daneel is now with this young girl.

“How are you, Leandro?”

“I see you found a new companion.  What’s her name?” muttered Leandro with great effort.

“Alyssa,” Daneel answered and continued “We had a good life together, didn’t we?”

“Yeah… I believed what doctors said. I was  convinced you were a figment of my imagination …  an alternate identity.  But I began doubting when DeeJay left and I could no longer sense your presence. I tried letting you takeover a few times but you didn’t.  Even then, I wasn’t sure if you were real.   But now you are here, brother.”

“I am.  Let me introduce you to Alyssa…” Daneel takes off the glasses Isaac gave him and allows Alyssa to come out.  

“Hi, Leo…” Alyssa said awkwardly.

“Hi…” Leandro said before he started coughing and took off the beanie hat from head and offered it to Alyssa.  Alyssa stepped back confused but Daneel took over, put on his glasses and accepted it. “You loved it when Mom gave it to you.”

“Take care of it….” as he gradually slipped away.  

Alyssa watched as Leandro's body went still. She felt a pang of sadness for this man she had never known, but she could sense the bond that he and Daneel had shared.  Standing at the foot of Leandro's bed, Alyssa looked lost.  He could sense her confusion and sadness.

The monitor displayed a flatline and started beeping.  Nurses rushed in -- pushing them away and ushered them out the room.

Daneel could feel the weight of Leandro's absence, a reminder of the fragility of human life. He wondered if she would be able to handle his consciousness. For now, they would have to live with the memory of Leandro, the man who had become a part of their shared consciousness.

"Let’s go."

They walked in silence, lost in their thoughts. Alyssa had a million questions, but she didn't know where to start. Daneel could sense her curiosity.

As they strolled through the park, Alyssa's mind drifted in sorrow, Daneel led her to a bench. They sat down, and Daneel took off the glasses, allowing her to regain control. Her gaze fixated on the ground, lost in a melancholic abyss. She asked Daneel, "Did he die in pain?"

Daneel put on the glasses and stood up..

People walked their dogs, children played on the swings, and the sound of laughter filled the air.  

Life goes on.
