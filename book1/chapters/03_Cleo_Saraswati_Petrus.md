# Chapter 3: Cleo Saraswati & Petrus
### Cleo

The Global Tracking System finally found the unknown female in General Beacon Hospital, Manhattan: Cleo Saraswati, the 17-year old daughter of Shahkar and Mira Saraswati.  She had been reported missing five  years ago.  She was thought to be dead. Her parents  have already been notified that she has been found.

She had Stage-5 infection but was rendered non-infectious by the hospital.

When Eli entered the room, he noticed a boy sitting in  the corner --wearing an outfit similar to the patient’s.  His eyes were closed, possibly meditating.  Cleo was drifting in and out of consciousness, mumbling.  But as Eli came to her bedside, she opened her eyes, sat up and removed the oxygen mask.

“I am glad you … are not infected.., Mr. Washington..,” she spoke with an effort.  “Do you mind if I call you… Lije?”

“Ms. Saraswati, nobody calls me Lije.  Have we met?   Why were you in Old Manhattan?  And more importantly, why did you drag me out of the Museum?”

“I have been following you since… we first met, Lije…” she continued.

“Please don’t call me Lije.  Cleo, your parents have been notified…”

“Please call me Daneel..” 

His AI informed him that the name Daneel is frequently used as a figurehead title in addressing leaders in the Foundation.

“Did the Foundation force you to join its organization when you disappeared? . .   Your parents are on their way, they’ ll be here in a fewhours.  Now...”  

"Di mian kwa ee,..," she muttered and began stuttering "...wa ho…seh" 

He didn’t understand her   but the phrase sounded familiar.  He gave her a puzzled look and said, “You are severely infected.  Rest for now.  I’ll come back later when your parents are here and you have rested.

Interrupting Eli, Cleo coughed, cleared her throat and slowly whispered the same phrase.  Eli leaned towards her and listened carefully.  Eli had heard that phrase before.  He was then yanked back to the time when he was 11 years old -- and suddenly remembered those were the last words from his *Whaea*.  Words he didn't understand then and still had no idea what they meant.

“Wha.. what?  What are you saying,...” Eli asked.

The boy from the corner suddenly appeared beside Cleo.  He touched the pendant around her neck.  It glowed briefly and detached itself.  The young man then attached it to his own necklace.  Her body convulsed and she faded into a coma. 

“  What does it mean? Tell me!” Eli demanded as he gripped her arms and shook her violently. 

"*Di mian kwa ee, wa ho seh*.  It means ‘don’t worry, I’m alright’ in Hokkien -- an old Southern Chinese language that has not been spoken in the last couple of centuries.”  

“That was what Charlene Kapetaua, your grandmother whom you call ‘*Whaea’* -- said before she died," the boy continued.  

Even before the boy finished speaking, Eli released his grip on Cleo and turned to the boy.  The System couldn’t identify the young male in front of him and so he asked “Who are you? ”

“I am Daneel, Lije, as I’ve said.  This is my new vessel, Petrus van Wyk.”  The boy slowly tapped his chest with his finger.
