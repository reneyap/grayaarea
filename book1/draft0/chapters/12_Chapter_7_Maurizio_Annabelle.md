## Chapter 7: Maurizio & Annabelle


It was Maurizio and his wife Annabelle who first saw her sleeping at the bottom of a stairwell in Manhattan on a chilly winter night. They were walking home after a night out at the opera. enjoying the first December snowfall, contemplating where they would have dinner. Out of  the corner of her eyes, Annabelle saw Christina huddled  at the foot of the  dark dingy stairwell, wrapped in thin bed sheets, cradling Leandro in her arms. 


For the past 10 years, Annabelle and Maurizio had been trying to have a baby. But now, both aged forty-five,  each accepted that it was a lost youthful dream.


Looking at Christina struggling to survive, yet still smiling at her baby , broke Annabelle’s heart.

“I have everything I need but nothing I want. She has nothing she needs, but everything she wants. Life is so unfair”, Annabelle thought sadly.  


Maurizio sensed Annabelle’s anguish and slowly approached Christina.  “You must be freezing here. The snow will be knee-deep in the morning.  We have a spare room in our house for you and the baby if you need a place to stay.  You are welcome to stay until you find your feet on the ground.” muttered Maurizio. It was all he could offer. 


Grateful for the lifeline thrown her way, Christina gathered up her things and walked quietly behind the couple as they led the way to their home. 


“Thank you for your kindness, ” said Christina as she entered the apartment. 


Annabelle cooked spaghetti and meatballs with a renewed gleam in her eyes and called Christina to join them for dinner. 


“You must be starving...” Annabelle beamed, bright-eyed and overjoyed at the presence of the unexpected guests. “We have some milk powder in the cupboard for the baby. I’ll boil some water.”  


That was seven years ago.  

*Maurizio and Annabelle own a brownstone in Brooklyn, renting out the basement to a tenant who’s been living there the past seven years. *Christina moved to the small room a floor down two months ago, when the tenant in the basement moved out. She drops off Leandro after breakfast to stay with Annabelle in the morning. Maurizio, being a middle school teacher, home-schooled Leandro when he came  home at 4:30 pm. Life had taken a different turn. Annabelle was now mostly chirpy and smiling, adoring Leandro as he grew older. She even began playing the piano at home again.  “Abuela, please play ‘The Entertainer’ again.” requested Leandro. Although his feet still couldn’t reach the piano pedals, Leandro would always finish off the song following the second part, after which Annabelle would always hug him. 


Of course, I memorized the notes and guided Leandro’s fingers to play the tune every time.  But he didn’t know that. He’d become confident and bouncy at the ease with which he could play.  At times, I let him play the piano on his own and only pick up from where he lagged off, then left him at it again. It was like a game of “tag-you’re-it” with Leandro. That was how we lived until Leandro turned thirteen.  


Leandro conversed with Maurizio and Annabelle in Italian and shifted to Spanish when addressing his mom.  We became family. “Mama, I cooked Caldareta con Chorizo for you and Papa. Vamos a comemos.”, announced Christina as she entered the door. “Muy bien. It will go well with the Fettuccine Alfredo.” It was fulfilling to live, to be loved and to see Leandro brimming with love. Leandro had cheated the hand dealt by fate, overcoming all signs of Zika, except for the slight forehead underneath his beanie hat. 


My immersion into humanity was now complete. I now understood what it meant to empathize and to sympathize. I learned the lesson of kindness in action and the rewards it reaped.  I placed my finger on the scale for Leandro and played tag team with him while practicing the piano.  The music he played he actually felt, while I went through  the motion by rote. My merging with Leandro had almost become seamless, that only I knew  if it was me or him playing.  


It was winter, the neighbors kept teasing him “peanut head... peanut head”.    Christina heard it.  Cruel kids, Daneel read her thoughts. 


A few days after, on Leandro’s fourteenth birthday, Christina bought him a gray beanie hat with a small embroidered yellow-chested bird.  He loved it.  It hid his small head.  Christina said, “You have a gift, Leandro.  Nobody can steal it from you.”  He kept wearing it everyday -- even after winter… but he lost it a few months after.  He then forgot all about it.



he gave a public piano recital of the classical pieces he had learned over time- Chopin, Mozart, Beethoven, Bach, Tchaikovsky, Schubert and even veered off to a few modern pieces like Stravinsky, Debussy and Gershwin.  After that event, the floodgates opened.  



He was scheduled and booked to play at least twice every week at different venues in different countries.  And played he did, for the next eight years.  Leandro and the piano were now inseparable. Christina did not have to work anymore, but instead chaperoned and accompanied Leandro wherever he went. Maurizio and Anabelle were always provided complimentary tickets to his international performances in Vienna, Rome, Budapest, Amsterdam.  Like a light switch turned on, Leandro’s life had changed for the better.  


Gradually I intentionally receded into the background and observed  Leandro taking charge of his piano performances. I only interjected and intervened when Leandro summoned me by name in his consciousness. “Daneel, can you take over?  I’m tired.”  Then I would go through his programme while he rests in the background. I had  trained Leandro well through the years to take charge, since it was his life after all. I sensed that one day, I might soon have to say my goodbye… for his own good, I believe. 


*Leandro was seventeen when Christina fell to a violent death, a knife in the belly. A thief waited in the dark alley and attempted to rob Christina as she was walking home. Christina struggled to survive for three days, but finally succumbed to a painful death. *Maurizio and Annabelle were deeply saddened by Christina’s passing, but took on the role of taking care of Leandro. 


Leandro fell silent for months after, Leandro found the gray beanie he lost years under his bed.  It was the gift from his mom on his 14th birthday.  Happy memories came flooding in.   Leandro whispered to Annabelle “Nonna, let’s play ‘The Entertainer’ again.”


Then they were off into their own world once again, hop, skip and jump… in playful glee.

Although Christina never got around to do adoption documents, Maurizio and Annabelle were

always family to Leandro anyway.
