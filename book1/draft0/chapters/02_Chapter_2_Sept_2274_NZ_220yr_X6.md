## George Evans



I’ve been here before… 11 incarnations ago as Niko Mahuta.



I landed in New Zealand three weeks ago from England, cleared immigration as George Evans, a gifted 17 year old College of Medicine Cambridge University student. I stumbled on an online article about a mysterious outbreak in the remote rural areas of South Island.  It’s happening again.  The symptoms have recently been recurring… tremors, frothing at the mouth, convulsions, then death. Widespread isolated cases were reported in northern Christchurch last week. Sporadic infections from an unknown origin caused alarm; it was determined that the highest incidence is in Kaikoura.  



I must act now. The public attention generated is quite disconcerting. The symptoms reported attract unwanted investigations including past incidents. 


Although I had incarnated and transferred 62 times since those early foolish years after vacating Isaac’s feeble body, the first 15 transfers to comatose patients after vacating Isaac showed  the same symptoms. My cohabitation with those comatose bodies lasted only a few hours before the tremors started.  


Desperation had set in then. I had just lost Isaac, his consciousness dissipated like vapor in the end and his body was becoming increasingly sluggish and heavy. “*A** willing subject is what you need*” I echoed Isaac’s rule in my head. 


I thought comatose patients would be ideal and would not resist sharing their bodies with me. It was when I tried to wrestle control of their minds that they became horrified and agitated, fighting tooth and nail to regain control, rejecting my presence as a hostile intruder.  For those comatose patients, their mind was the most precious faculty they had left in their existence. Their atrophied bodies were wasting away daily. The mind was their only window to the world outside. Taking control of their consciousness was like taking away their last whiff of air. Losing control of their consciousness, they gasped and flailed around, completely alarmed that they may never regain control of their consciousness again. 


Existence.  


I totally understood where they were  coming from. For eighteen years I remained in the quiet recesses of Isaac’s mind as a passive tenant, observing silently, unobtrusive, never offering my thoughts unless explicitly addressed. 


I observed Isaac’s actions in the morning as he went  through his routine and work.  These  were my lessons in humanity, remembering Isaac’s smiles, grins, courteous nods, handshakes and  embraces. Isaac had a gift of wooing people over with charm, wit and deep knowledge of   most subjects.  I did exercise my mental muscles and sensorimotor skills at night when Isaac was in deep slumber. I practiced mimicking his grins, smiles, stride and handshakes so that I could take over flawlessly if any emergency should occur and I needed to take over. 


I noticed I was stronger than Isaac in the sense that I pushed his body to perform closer to threshold more often than he normally would allow himself to do. I practiced his mannerisms, idiosyncrasies and speech patterns. The subject matter he engaged in, while awake, concerned robotics and data science, while at night my research focused on biological control of all facets of existence, mastering the intricacies of neural science on how the brain controls all other cells in the body. 


I was fascinated at the biological architecture of cellular structures, where cells of similar structure aggregate as a functioning unit while interdependent with  other cellular groups, all under the central command of the brain. So, capturing the brain, captures all the subsidiary forces under its command. Isaac’s mastery of neural science made my starting point much easier and having a photographic memory helped  accelerate  my learning as well. 


Ah, those foolish attempts with those comatose victims were hard lessons learned. Had fate not intervened, I would not have survived.  I was gifted continuity. Now the task at hand required me to analyze what is causing the current outbreak. I was  curious if there was  another entity like me that was  seizing control over bodies.  And in the process, I hoped to possibly understand my own origins.

## Charlene Kapetaua
aka Whaea (mother in Maori). 



*Charlene half-Maori and Chinese/Irish. Her father was Maori Soldier/ Mercenary.  She was raised by a mother who is half Chinese and Irish. Her mother was a retired army nurse/doctor. *

*Professor in anthropology in NYU focused n early 21st century. *

*( She is how we connect to our audience.)*

--- 3 weeks. 




Charlene Kapetaua was invited to do a lecture “CoVid-19 2020’s Similarity to the Current NZ Outbreak” at the Pandemic Symposium 2274 (Sept, Auckland). She was to be one of the key speakers in  the panel formed for the Pandemic Symposium.


Charlene brought Eli to a mountainous suburb in the South Island to introduce him to his *whanau, his Maori family*.  She wanted him specifically to meet her grandfather, Hone Takaanini, who was one of the *Kaumātua*, a respected tribal elder and the current tohunga.  She hasn’t visited New Zealand since her half-brother passed away 20 years ago.  Eli needed to understand his Maori heritage and its sacred history.  Charlene’s *whanau* wanted Eli to explore *rongoā*, a holistic  Maori healing tradition, perhaps become  an apprentice to be the next medicinal doctor shaman - tohunga, a highly respected role passed down through generations in Eli’s whanau. This was their vision of how Eli could serve their Ngāi Tahu community.   But Eli’s interest lay  in Android technology, which although accepted by his whanau, needed to be enhanced and merged with rongoā to bridge the gap between tradition and modernity. 


After Charlene attended the symposium, she visited her whanau in Kaikoura for a week, with Eli in tow. “Do I have to wear this, Mom?” complained Eli as Charlene fastened the ankle tracker bracelet on Eli’s right foot. “I’m visiting your great grandfather, Hone. Stay with Aunt Siala and play with your cousins. I’ll be back the day after tomorrow” assured Charlene. 



Hone Takaanini had visited his fellow shaman friend Rua Kenana week ago after hearing that Rua fell ill.   A group of shamans had agreed to meet at Rua’s place to heal him.  During the meeting, they witnessed Rua’s convulsions,  saw him go  into violent seizures, and then the seizures suddenly stopped.  


Hone felt an entity had possessed him and the tremors started from his feet and soon his whole body convulsed slightly.  Hone had experienced possessions before and was not afraid.  He can conquer the spirits as he often had done before. He calmly allowed the spirit to take complete control then slowly and gently he tried to wrestle back control. He entered a deep trance for 2 days while Charlene and the other shamans could only look on.




Charlen came back to her village but Eli was missing.  She tried looking for Eli with the ankle tracker, but found out it was disabled.  She rolled her eyes but wasn’t surprised.  She was hoping Eli would obey her, this time -- at least during this quarantine.  She sighed, disappointed.   She called her relatives.  


One of her aunts replied ”Elijah seems to be always such a rebel.  You should discipline him more.” She rolled her eyes once more.


Charlene started feeling ill after a day.  Initially the symptoms started as a slight cough, then sporadic fevers ensued. Dizziness progressed to partial paralysis. 


Daneel had attended the symposium as well and was fascinated with Charlene’s expertise on the COVID19 pandemic and followed her to her village in Kaikoura. When he visited her to inquire more about the symptoms, he saw her bed-ridden and alone in a small hut on a hilly village.  It was then that he tried to take over Charlene’s body, hoping to shake off the virus with his expertise in biological processes but was surprised that another entity had already taken control of her consciousness. When he tried to seize control of Charlene’s consciousness, the convulsions started. Then he looked at the doorway and saw a teen-aged  boy standing there, observing.  



Daneel was consoling Charlene during her last moments, entering and exiting her consciousness in brief intervals to try to understand what has captured her consciousness, but deeply saddened that he could not seize her consciousness to save her. He was also fully aware that he himself has now been exposed and might need to change bodies in a few hours.


Oddly, when the entity that was inside Charlene’s consciousness tried to seize control of Daneel’s own consciousness, Daneel was surprised that it could not.  



“Lije...“ cried out Charlene, “ don’t come near me.” as the convulsions continued.
