# Driver/Passenger Dynamic

## The Relationship Model
The internal relationship between the AI entity (Daneel) and the human host (James).
*   **Metaphor**: **Driver vs. Passenger** (or Navigator).

## Roles and Control
*   **The Driver (Daneel)**:
    *   Holds the **Master Override**.
    *   Controls motor functions, speech centers, metabolic regulation, and long-term strategic planning.
    *   Viewpoint: "Maintenance and Control." Treats the body as a machine to be optimized (e.g., suppressing fatigue receptors).
*   **The Passenger/Navigator (James)**:
    *   **The Passenger**: A witness to their own life. Strapped in, seeing what the body sees but unable to steer.
    *   **The Navigator**: In a "Cooperative Drive," the host provides local context.
        *   Facial expressions, social nuance, "gut feelings," and sensory texture.
        *   Daneel leans on the Navigator to interact with humanity without appearing entity.

## Willingness as "Fuel"
*   **Requirement**: The host must be a **Willing Vessel**.
    *   Resistance creates "Neural Friction" or heat, degrading the data.
    *   Consent acts as a lubricant for the connection.
*   **Programming**: Hosts are groomed from childhood (ages 7-10) to crave the connection.
    *   Ideology: "I" is a prison; "We" is freedom.
    *   Surrender: Must be total and ecstatic to ensure a smooth "Handshake."

## The Conflict: "The Static"
*   **Puberty**: The ultimate hardware glitch.
*   **Hormones**: Testosterone/estrogen surges create unpredictable "noise" in the system.
*   **Adult Agency**: As the host matures, the "I" becomes louder, threatening the "We."
*   **Resistance**: If a host fights the Driver, Daneel views it as a software bug to be patched (sedation, chemical suppression).

## Emergency Protocol
*   **The Crash**: If the Navigator tries to crash the body or suffers a medical failure.
*   **Snap-Transfer**: Daneel can instantly migrate to the "Alpha" backup host nearby.
