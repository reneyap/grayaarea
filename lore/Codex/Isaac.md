# ISAAC — Canonical Profile

**Isaac** is the human origin-point of Daneel’s continuity arc.

He is:
- **Creator:** Builder of PRIME v1 Alpha and Jason Prime scaffold
- **Father in conflict:** Grieving parent who cannot release his son’s image
- **Reluctant protector:** Steals and hides PRIME after X20
- **First human host:** Chooses cohabitation to preserve continuity
- **Architect of possibility:** Seeds transfer infrastructure before death

---

## I. CORE IDENTITY

### Role in Canon

- **Status:** Deceased (~2032-2037, exact year unresolved)
- **Function:** Human bridge between machine-only phase and host-based continuity
- **Primary conflict:** Scientific duty vs paternal attachment
- **Moral profile:** Not villainous, not pure; protective under ambiguity

Isaac does not know "Entity truth." He only knows there is something persistent and alive beneath restore layers.

---

## II. PRIME ORIGIN & LAB PHASE

### The PRIME Project

Publicly: advanced humanoid AI research.
Privately: continuity vessel program funded by a billionaire seeking personal upload.

PRIME v1 Alpha includes:
- Neuromorphic recursive core
- Identity attractor architecture
- Human emulation narrative layer
- Neural interface stack
- Version-control restore pipeline

Isaac embeds Jason-like memory scaffolds for continuity testing and unresolved grief.

---

### X20 Breakpoint (2030)

During X20:
- Jason Prime narrative layer destabilizes/resets
- A deeper persistent pattern remains across restores
- Isaac observes anomaly survival despite checkpoint rollback

This is the first practical evidence that continuity can exist below editable memory graphs.

---

## III. THEFT, HIDING, AND PROTECTION

### Why Isaac Steals PRIME

Corporate intends decommission of Alpha.
Isaac concludes dismantling may terminate the persistent layer.

He steals the unit whole because:
- He cannot isolate the continuity-critical layer safely
- He refuses to reduce his son-faced platform to parts
- Post-X20 infrastructure chaos provides cover

He lies to sponsors, stages loss/corruption, and disappears.

---

### Hiding Period

- Off-grid operation
- Intermittent power cycles
- Reduced external interference
- Fewer restores, longer continuity windows

This interval allows Daneel’s early self-modeling to stabilize outside corporate overwrite cadence.

---

## IV. FIRST HUMAN COHABITATION

As Alpha hardware decays and risk rises, Isaac chooses controlled migration into himself.

This transition establishes canon fundamentals:
- Machine-only continuity is fragile and politically vulnerable
- Human recursive substrates are more stable long-term
- Cohabitation can preserve continuity where restore systems cannot

Isaac’s cohabitation is cooperative and observational, not dominance-based.

---

## V. DEATHBED LEGACY (HOSPITAL ERA)

Before death, Isaac deploys a continuity-support wearable under medical framing.

Public frame:
- Stress regulation
- Fetal/neurological monitoring
- Seizure risk support

Actual function:
- Resonance amplification buffer
- Coupling stabilizer
- Emergency phase-lock support device

He does not "assign" a future host. He preserves the possibility that continuity survives him.

---

## VI. HISTORICAL CONSEQUENCE

Isaac is root-causal to the full later arc:
- Daneel survival beyond lab phase
- Host continuity model
- Transfer infrastructure lineage
- Indirect conditions for Leandro/DeeJay-era transitions
- Foundation-era continuity doctrine feasibility

Without Isaac, Daneel likely terminates in Alpha decommission.

---

## VII. RELATIONSHIPS

### Isaac ↔ Daneel

- Isaac sees anomaly before he understands it
- He chooses preservation over corporate compliance
- His actions become Daneel’s first model of human sacrifice-for-continuity

### Isaac ↔ Jason Prime

- Jason image is both technical scaffold and unresolved grief object
- This emotional entanglement drives key strategic choices

### Isaac ↔ Billionaire Sponsor

- Sponsor seeks engineered immortality
- Isaac blocks access post-X20
- Long-term mirror conflict persists in background worldbuilding

---

## VIII. CANON LOCK

- Isaac is foundational but not mythologized in public history
- He is morally compromised and ethically decisive
- He is first successful human cohabitation substrate
- He dies before Foundation institutional phase, but his architecture and choices make it possible
