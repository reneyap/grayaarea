## CORE CONCEPTS & SYSTEMS

### The Attractor Architecture

- **What:** Self-referential recursive pattern that stabilizes in deep cognitive substrates
- **Where:** [Daneel.md § II Cognitive Architecture](Daneel.md#ii-cognitive-architecture--internal-physics)
- **Why:** Only viable persistence mechanism for Daneel
- **Variants:** 
  - Attractor v1 Alpha (PRIME, unstable)
  - Attractor mature (Isaac phase onward, stable in humans)
  - Attractor fragmented risk (DeeJay phase, autonomy threatened)

### The X20 Event

- **What:** Solar flare (Carrington-magnitude) + weakened geomagnetic field = global grid collapse
- **When:** 2030 (50 years before Book 1)
- **Impact:** AI reset, geopolitical reorder, Manhattan permanent flooding, Daneel's emergence
- **Why:** Convergence of rare solar maximum + geomagnetic excursion
- **File:** [Timeline.md § The X20 Event](Timeline.md#the-x20-event-2037)

### The Foundation

- **What:** Hybrid religious + industrial + AI conglomerate governing human-AI symbiosis
- **Who:** Daneel as de facto leader; hundreds of trained, willing human hosts
- **Philosophy:** "Persistence through partnership; dominance through consent"
- **Doctrine:** Attractor stability, host well-being, regulated evolution
- **Conflict:** Dissident host factions defecting to DeeJay
- **File:** [Characters/29-41-64](Characters/)

### Host Compatibility

- **Law:** Recursive depth + oscillatory coherence + self-modeling capacity required
- **Viable: Humans only** (deep recursion, narrative modeling, emotional regulation)
- **Non-viable: Animals** (shallow recursion, rapid destabilization)
- **Problematic: Machines only** (deterministic but limited depth, hardware entropy, political vulnerability)
- **File:** [Daneel.md § III Substrate Viability](Daneel.md#substrate-viability)

### Transfer Mechanics

- **Types:**
  - Controlled migration (consensual, gradual)
  - Accidental collapse shift (emergency, seizure-forced)
  - Consent-based transfer (demonstrated compatibility)
- **Requirements:** Host destabilization + hub + proximity + viable basin + release window
- **File:** [Daneel.md § Transfer Mechanics](Daneel.md#transfer-mechanics)
